FromSoftware, Inc.

FromSoftware, Inc. is a Japanese video game development company widely known for creating challenging, atmospheric games with deep and immersive worlds. The company was founded in November 1986 in Tokyo, Japan, by Naotoshi Zin, who originally established the studio as a developer of business and productivity software.

The Inception

FromSoftware first gained attention with the King’s Field series, released on the original PlayStation. These games introduced several elements that would later become core characteristics of the studio: dark fantasy settings, first-person exploration, high difficulty, and indirect storytelling. This design philosophy would eventually define the company’s future projects.

Improvement era

International recognition came with the release of Demon’s Souls in 2009. Although initially considered a niche title, the game became highly influential and laid the foundation for the “Soulslike” genre. Its success was followed by the critically acclaimed Dark Souls trilogy, which cemented FromSoftware’s reputation for creating demanding yet rewarding gameplay experiences. This was the time when Hidetaka Miyazaki was starting to make a big diference on FromSoftware.

FromSoftware Popularity

FromSoftware has become one of the most respected and influential video game studios in the world. Its global popularity skyrocketed after the release of Demon's Souls, which introduced a challenging combat system and dark, atmospheric storytelling. The studio solidified its reputation with the Dark Souls series, which popularized the “Soulslike” genre. These games are known for their high difficulty, deep lore, interconnected world design, and rewarding gameplay. Today, FromSoftware is celebrated for its unique design philosophy, loyal fanbase, and major impact on modern game development.
